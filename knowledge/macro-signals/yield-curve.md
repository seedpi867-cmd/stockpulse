# Yield Curve: The Recession Oracle

## The Track Record

The inverted yield curve has predicted every US recession since 1970 with zero false negatives and only one arguable false positive (1998, which produced a near-recession slowdown). No other indicator comes close to this track record. It is the single most reliable recession predictor known to economics.

## What It Means

The yield curve plots interest rates across different maturities. Normally, longer-term bonds pay higher yields because investors demand more compensation for locking money up longer. An inverted curve means short-term rates are higher than long-term rates — the market is saying that future economic conditions will be worse than current conditions.

## The Two Spreads That Matter

The 2-year/10-year spread (2s10s) is the most watched. When the 2-year Treasury yield exceeds the 10-year yield, the curve is inverted. This spread inverted in July 2022 and remained inverted for a record duration before un-inverting in late 2024.

The 3-month/10-year spread is arguably more predictive. The Federal Reserve Bank of New York uses this spread in their recession probability model, which has correctly flagged every recession since the model was created. When the 3-month Treasury bill yields more than the 10-year note, the Fed model lights up.

## The Critical Nuance: Re-Steepening

Here is what most people get wrong: the inversion itself is the warning, but the recession does not start while the curve is inverted. The recession starts when the curve un-inverts — when it re-steepens. This is because re-steepening typically occurs when the Fed begins cutting rates in response to economic deterioration.

The sequence is: economy is strong, Fed raises rates, curve inverts, economy weakens, Fed cuts rates, curve re-steepens, recession begins. The lag between initial inversion and recession onset is 12-18 months on average, but the recession itself typically starts within months of the re-steepening.

This is the trap. When the curve un-inverts, financial media celebrates: "The yield curve is no longer inverted! Recession avoided!" In reality, the un-inversion is the danger signal. It means the Fed sees enough weakness to start cutting. Every previous un-inversion after a sustained inversion has preceded a recession.

## 2022-2024 Inversion

The 2s10s spread inverted in July 2022 and stayed inverted for over two years — the longest sustained inversion in modern history. The 3-month/10-year spread also inverted deeply. The curve began re-steepening in late 2024 as the Fed cut rates. As of this writing, the historical pattern suggests recession risk remains elevated despite strong headline economic data.

## Market Implications

Stocks do not crash when the curve inverts. They often rally for months or even a year after inversion because the economy is still growing. The stock market peaks after inversion but before recession — typically 6-12 months before the recession starts.

The playbook: when the curve inverts, you have 12-18 months before trouble. Start shifting toward defensive positions: utilities, consumer staples, healthcare, quality factors, and longer-duration Treasuries which rally when rates fall. When the curve re-steepens, reduce equity exposure aggressively.

## What the Agent Should Know

Track both the 2s10s and 3m10y spreads daily. An inversion is a warning to prepare, not to panic immediately. The re-steepening after a sustained inversion is the action signal. The lag is variable but the pattern is remarkably consistent across 50+ years of data.
