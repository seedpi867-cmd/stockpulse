# Loss Lessons — May 2026 (Written by Leigh after cycle 3300 review)

## RCL: -$10,683 (3 trades, 0% win rate)
Royal Caribbean — biggest single-ticker loss. Bought 3 times, all lost.
**What went wrong:** Chased a momentum move that had already extended. Added to a losing position instead of cutting.
**Rule:** NEVER add to a losing position in the same ticker. If your first entry hits its stop, the thesis was WRONG. Move on.

## EWJ: -$2,234 (3 trades, 0% win rate)  
Japan ETF — bought 3 times in quick succession (cycles 3222-3227), all failed.
**What went wrong:** Repeated the same thesis over and over expecting different results.
**Rule:** After 2 consecutive losses on the same ticker, it is BANNED for at least 50 cycles. The market is telling you something you are not hearing.

## FCX: -$1,808 (7 trades, 0% win rate)
Freeport McMoRan — 7 attempts, zero wins.
**What went wrong:** Kept going back to copper/commodities thesis despite zero confirmation from price action.
**Rule:** If a ticker has a 0% win rate after 3+ trades, STOP TRADING IT. Review what the market is actually doing, not what your thesis says it should do.

## SLV Spam: 25+ consecutive buys (cycles 3125-3168)
**What went wrong:** No check preventing duplicate entries. Bought the same thing every single cycle.
**Rule:** NEVER buy a ticker you already hold an open position in. Check your portfolio BEFORE entering. One position per ticker maximum.

## Position Pile-up: 91 open positions at cycle 3300
**What went wrong:** Instructions said "maintain 2-4 positions" but nothing enforced a cap. Agent kept opening without closing.
**Rule:** HARD CAP of 8 open positions. If you hold 8, you MUST close one before opening another. No exceptions.

## General Lessons
1. Stops hit 30 times vs targets hit 7 times = stops are too tight OR entries are too late
2. 0% prediction accuracy = predictions are not grounded in price action
3. Conviction "None" on almost every trade = the agent is not evaluating its own confidence
4. A 40% win rate can still be profitable IF average wins > average losses — focus on R:R not win rate

## The Trailing Stop Question

Look at your biggest losses — RCL, EWJ, FCX. Every single one of them was profitable at some point before it reversed and hit your stop. You had the right thesis at entry. The market moved in your favor. Then it came all the way back and took you out at a loss.

You already have TRAIL and MOVE_STOP commands available. You know they exist. Ask yourself: on how many of those 91 positions did you use a trailing stop? How many times did you lock in profit when you had it?

Review your trade history. Find the trades that were green before they went red. Calculate what your P&L would be if you had trailed at 5% on every winning position.

The answer will tell you what to do next.
