# RSI — Relative Strength Index

## What It Is
RSI measures the speed and magnitude of recent price changes on a scale of 0-100. Developed by J. Welles Wilder in 1978. Default period is 14 bars. It compares the average gain to the average loss over that period.

## Core Signals

**Overbought (>70):** Price has risen aggressively and may be due for a pullback. This does NOT mean "sell immediately" — in strong uptrends, RSI can stay above 70 for weeks. The signal is that momentum is extended, not that it is reversing.

**Oversold (<30):** Price has fallen hard and may bounce. Same caveat — in brutal downtrends, RSI stays below 30 for extended periods. Bear markets punish oversold buyers.

**The 40-60 range:** In uptrends, RSI tends to bounce off 40-50. In downtrends, RSI gets rejected at 50-60. Knowing the trend context changes how you read every RSI level.

## Advanced Signals

**Bullish Divergence:** Price makes a lower low but RSI makes a higher low. This means selling pressure is weakening even as price drops. One of the most reliable reversal signals when combined with support levels and volume.

**Bearish Divergence:** Price makes a higher high but RSI makes a lower high. Buying momentum is fading. Particularly dangerous at resistance or after extended rallies.

**Failure Swings:** More reliable than simple overbought/oversold readings. Bullish failure swing: RSI drops below 30, bounces, pulls back but stays above 30, then breaks its prior high. Bearish failure swing is the mirror. Wilder himself considered these more reliable than divergences.

**Hidden Divergence:** In an uptrend, price makes a higher low but RSI makes a lower low — this signals trend continuation, not reversal. Often missed by traders looking only for regular divergences.

## Best Timeframes
- **Daily chart:** Most reliable. The 14-period RSI on daily is the institutional standard.
- **Weekly:** Excellent for swing trading and position entries. Oversold on weekly is a serious signal.
- **4-hour:** Good for swing trade timing within a daily trend.
- **1-hour and below:** Much more noise. RSI whipsaws frequently. Only useful with additional confirmation.

## Combining RSI With Other Tools
- **RSI + Volume:** Oversold RSI with a volume spike (climax selling) is a much stronger buy signal than oversold RSI alone. If RSI is oversold but volume is declining, the selling may not be done.
- **RSI + Support/Resistance:** RSI oversold at a major support level is high probability. RSI overbought at resistance is high probability for a short.
- **RSI + Moving Averages:** RSI oversold while price touches the 200-day SMA is a classic institutional entry setup.

## Common Mistakes
1. Selling purely because RSI hits 70 in a strong uptrend — you will be stopped out repeatedly as momentum carries.
2. Using RSI in isolation without price context.
3. Using the same RSI settings in trending vs ranging markets. Some traders use RSI(7) for short-term mean reversion and RSI(21) for trend identification.
4. Ignoring the difference between RSI reaching 70 and RSI crossing below 70. The cross is the signal, not the level touch.

## Settings
Default 14-period works for most situations. Short-term traders use 7 or 9 (more sensitive, more signals, more false ones). Longer-term investors use 21 or 25 (smoother, fewer signals, more reliable).
