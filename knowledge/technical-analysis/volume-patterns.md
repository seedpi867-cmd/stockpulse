# Volume Patterns — Confirmation, Climax, and Accumulation

## Volume as Confirmation
Volume is the fuel behind price movement. Price moves on high volume are more meaningful than moves on low volume. This is the single most important principle in technical analysis that most retail traders ignore.

**Uptrend health:** In a healthy uptrend, volume should expand on up days and contract on down days. This shows institutions are buying into strength and sitting out pullbacks. When this pattern reverses — heavy volume on down days, light volume on up days — the trend is sick even if price has not broken yet.

**Downtrend health:** In a healthy downtrend, volume expands on down days and contracts on rallies. Heavy volume on rally days within a downtrend signals potential accumulation and a possible trend change.

## Climax Volume

**Selling climax:** A massive spike in volume accompanied by a sharp price drop, often on a gap down. This represents capitulation — the last holders giving up in panic. Selling climaxes often mark bottoms or at least tradeable bounces. The volume spike is key — it must be 3-5x or more than the average daily volume.

**Buying climax:** A massive volume spike on a sharp rally, often near the end of an extended uptrend. This represents euphoric buying — everyone who wanted to buy has bought. Buying climaxes often mark tops. The January 2021 peak in many meme stocks showed textbook buying climax patterns.

**Identifying climax volume:** Look for volume bars that are dramatically taller than anything in the prior weeks. If the prior 20-day average volume is 5 million shares and today trades 25 million, that is potentially climactic.

## Volume Dry-Up Before Breakouts

One of the most reliable setups in technical analysis: price consolidates in a tight range while volume progressively decreases. This pattern — sometimes called a "volume contraction" — signals that selling pressure is exhausted and buying pressure is building. When price finally breaks out of the range, it often does so explosively.

**The pattern:**
1. Price forms a base or consolidation (could be a triangle, flag, or rectangle).
2. Volume declines each week/day as the pattern develops.
3. On the breakout day, volume surges to confirm the move.
4. A breakout without a volume surge is suspect and more likely to fail.

## Accumulation and Distribution

**Accumulation:** Institutions buying large positions over days or weeks. They cannot buy all at once without moving the price against themselves. Signs: price holds steady or drifts slightly while volume is above average. Small-range days on high volume near support = institutions absorbing supply.

**Distribution:** Institutions selling into strength. Signs: price stalls at highs despite above-average volume. Each rally attempt shows high volume but price does not advance much — supply is overwhelming demand. Distribution precedes most major tops by weeks or months.

**Wyckoff method:** Richard Wyckoff formalized these patterns in the 1930s. Accumulation has distinct phases: selling climax, automatic rally, secondary test, spring (shakeout below support), then markup. Distribution mirrors this with a buying climax, automatic reaction, upthrust (fake breakout above resistance), then markdown.

## On-Balance Volume (OBV)

Created by Joe Granville. Simple concept: add the entire days volume if price closes up, subtract if price closes down. The absolute number does not matter — the trend of OBV matters.
