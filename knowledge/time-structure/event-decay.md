# Event Decay — Every Catalyst Has a Half-Life

## The Principle

The market impact of any event decays over time. A Federal Reserve rate decision moves markets intensely for 2 days, modestly for a week, and is largely absorbed within 2 weeks. An earnings miss moves a stock for 1-2 weeks (with drift extending to 60-90 days per PEAD, see earnings-drift.md). A recession changes the market environment for 6-12 months. A structural technological shift (the internet, smartphones, AI) reshapes markets for years.

Every catalyst has a half-life — a period over which its impact decays by 50%. Understanding event decay is critical because most traders hold too long for short-lived events and not long enough for structural ones.

## Event Decay Taxonomy

### Flash Events (Half-life: hours to 1 day)
- Flash crashes
- Individual stock halts
- Intraday technical breakdowns/breakouts without fundamental catalyst
- Social media rumors (before/after verification)

Trading implication: these events are noise unless they reveal underlying structural issues. Do not react unless you have reason to believe the flash event is symptomatic of something deeper. Most flash events fully reverse within 24-48 hours.

### Short-Duration Events (Half-life: 1-5 days)
- Federal Reserve rate decisions (when in line with expectations)
- Monthly economic data releases (jobs, CPI, PMI)
- Individual stock analyst upgrades/downgrades
- Geopolitical tensions that do not escalate (missile tests, diplomatic spats)
- Single-day market sell-offs without structural catalyst

Trading implication: if you are trading these events, your holding period should be measured in days, not weeks. Enter quickly, take profits quickly. Holding a rate-decision trade for 3 weeks means you are holding noise after the signal has decayed.

### Medium-Duration Events (Half-life: 1-4 weeks)
- Earnings announcements (initial impact, before drift)
- Central bank policy changes (rate hikes/cuts, QE announcements)
- Sector-specific regulatory actions
- M&A announcements
- Significant geopolitical developments (sanctions, trade policy changes)

Trading implication: these events justify positions held for 2-8 weeks. The initial reaction establishes direction; the subsequent drift (if any) provides the tradeable follow-through. Set time-based exits aligned with the event's decay rate.

### Long-Duration Events (Half-life: 2-6 months)
- Recessions (official or market-anticipated)
- Major policy regime changes (QE to QT transitions, significant fiscal stimulus)
- Commodity supply shocks (OPEC+ cuts, pipeline disruptions)
- Currency crises

Trading implication: these events justify positions held for months. Do not try to trade the daily noise within a long-duration event. Hold positions aligned with the event's direction and adjust at the macro level (quarterly or when new information materially changes the thesis).

### Structural Events (Half-life: 1-5+ years)
- Technological paradigm shifts (internet, mobile, AI)
- Demographic changes (aging populations, urbanization)
- Regulatory framework changes (Dodd-Frank, Sarbanes-Oxley)
- Globalization/de-globalization cycles

Trading implication: these events justify multi-year portfolio tilts, not individual trades. The noise around a structural event is enormous — weekly and monthly fluctuations obscure the multi-year trend. Patience is the only edge.

## The Mismatch Problem

The most common trading error related to event decay:

**Holding too long for short events.** A trader buys a stock after a positive CPI report (short-duration event). The stock rallies for 2 days. The trader holds for 3 weeks, waiting for more upside. The CPI impact has fully decayed by day 5, and the subsequent 2 weeks of price action have nothing to do with the original catalyst. The trader is now holding a position with no thesis.

**Holding too short for long events.** A trader buys energy stocks when OPEC+ announces production cuts (long-duration event). The position is up 5% in a week. The trader takes profits. Over the next 6 months, energy stocks rally another 25% as the supply reduction works through the system.

## For the Agent

- Classify every trade catalyst by its decay category before entering
- Set time-based exits aligned with the catalyst's half-life: position duration should be 2-3x the half-life
- When holding a position past its catalyst's decay period, require a NEW catalyst to justify continuation — otherwise exit
- Track historical decay rates for different event types to refine the taxonomy
- Never confuse a structural event (AI transformation) with a short-duration event (individual AI company earnings). They require different holding periods and different position sizes
