# Technical Plus Fundamental: When Both Agree, Conviction Is Maximum

## Core Principle

Technical analysis tells you what the market is doing. Fundamental analysis tells you what the market should be doing. When both agree, you have a high-conviction setup — price action confirms the fundamental thesis and the fundamental thesis explains the price action. When they disagree, one of them will eventually capitulate. On a 3-month horizon, fundamentals almost always win. On a 3-day horizon, technicals dominate. The collision between the two tells you both direction and timeframe.

## High-Conviction Setups (Agreement)

### Bullish Agreement
The stock is technically oversold (RSI below 30, testing support, high volume washout) AND the fundamentals are improving (insider buying, upward estimate revisions, strong balance sheet, sector tailwinds). This is the bread-and-butter setup for long positions.

**Why it works:** The price has been pushed down by short-term selling pressure (tax-loss harvesting, fund redemptions, panic) but the underlying business is getting better. The technical oversold condition means sellers are exhausted. The fundamental improvement means new buyers are coming. The rebound has both a technical catalyst (mean reversion) and a fundamental catalyst (value recognition).

**Signals to stack:**
- RSI below 30 on daily chart
- Price at or below 200-day SMA
- Insider buying cluster (3+ insiders in 30 days)
- Earnings estimate revisions trending upward
- Short interest declining (smart money covering)

### Bearish Agreement
The stock is breaking below technical support (200-day SMA, multi-month trendline, key horizontal level) AND the fundamentals are deteriorating (earnings misses, guidance cuts, insider selling, debt covenants at risk). This is the high-conviction short.

**Why it works:** The technical breakdown means institutional holders are liquidating. The fundamental deterioration means no one is stepping in to buy. The combination creates an air pocket below — no natural buyers until the price reaches a level where the (deteriorated) fundamentals offer deep value.

**Signals to stack:**
- Breaking below 200-day SMA with heavy volume
- Earnings miss with guidance cut
- Insider selling above normal patterns
- Debt-to-equity ratio rising
- Sector peers also showing weakness (not company-specific, systemic)

## The Disagreement Problem

### Strong Technicals + Deteriorating Fundamentals
The stock is in a beautiful uptrend, breaking out to new highs, momentum is strong — but the fundamentals are getting worse. Revenue growth is slowing, margins are compressing, the competitive position is weakening.

**Resolution:** The technicals can carry the stock for weeks to months on momentum alone. But eventually (the 3-month horizon), the fundamentals assert themselves. This is the classic setup where momentum traders make money on the way up and fundamental traders make money on the way down. If you are long on technicals, have a tight trailing stop. If you are short on fundamentals, be patient and wait for the technical breakdown to confirm.

### Weak Technicals + Improving Fundamentals
The stock is in a downtrend, making lower lows, technically ugly — but the business is quietly improving. New product launches, cost cuts, market share gains.

**Resolution:** This is the deep value setup. The fundamentals will eventually pull the stock higher, but the timing is uncertain. Do not fight the downtrend. Wait for a technical signal that the selling is exhausted: a higher low, a positive divergence on RSI (price makes new low but RSI makes a higher low), or a volume climax (massive selling volume followed by a reversal day).

## The Time Horizon Bridge

- **Day trading:** Technicals dominate. Fundamentals are irrelevant on a 4-hour candle.
- **Swing trading (1-4 weeks):** Both matter equally. The best swing trades have both.
- **Position trading (1-6 months):** Fundamentals dominate. Technicals are for entry timing only.
- **Investing (6+ months):** Only fundamentals matter. Any technical entry point is fine.

Match your analysis weighting to your holding period. A day trader using fundamental analysis is wasting effort. A long-term investor ignoring fundamentals for chart patterns is gambling.

## The Stacking Rule

The more independent signals that align (technical + fundamental + sentiment + positioning + macro), the higher your conviction should be. Two aligned signals = trade. Three aligned signals = size up. Four or more aligned signals = this is the trade you tell stories about later. But verify each signal is truly independent — two technical signals stacking are worth less than one technical and one fundamental signal stacking.
