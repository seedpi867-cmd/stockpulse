# 13F Filings — The Institutional X-Ray

## What It Is

Every institutional investment manager with over $100 million in qualifying assets under management must file SEC Form 13F within 45 calendar days of each quarter end. This is a legal requirement under Section 13(f) of the Securities Exchange Act of 1934. The filings disclose long equity positions, options, and convertible bonds. They do NOT disclose short positions, cash, fixed income, or foreign-only listed securities.

Filing deadlines: Q1 (March 31) due May 15. Q2 (June 30) due August 14. Q3 (September 30) due November 14. Q4 (December 31) due February 14.

## Who Files and Why It Matters

The biggest names in investing file 13Fs: Berkshire Hathaway (Buffett), Soros Fund Management, Duquesne (Druckenmiller), Pershing Square (Ackman), Bridgewater (Dalio), Renaissance Technologies (Simons estate), Citadel, Two Sigma, D.E. Shaw. Over 5,000 institutions file every quarter.

The raw positions are interesting but not the edge. Everyone can see Buffett holds Apple. That is old news by the time you read it.

## The Real Edge: Tracking Changes

The edge is in the DELTA between filings. What changed? What is new? What was sold entirely?

- **New positions** are the strongest signal. When Druckenmiller initiates a new position, he has done months of work you have not. A new position from a top manager is a concentrated thesis.
- **Significant increases** (>25% position growth) show conviction is rising.
- **Complete exits** mean the thesis is dead. If Soros dumps an entire position, something changed.
- **Concentration changes** matter more than absolute size. If a fund goes from 3% to 8% in one name, that is a high-conviction bet.

## The 45-Day Delay Problem

The data is stale. A filing for March 31 positions is not public until mid-May. The fund could have already reversed course. This is why 13F data is directional intelligence, not a trade signal. You are seeing where smart money WAS, not where it IS.

Mitigation: combine 13F data with real-time signals. If a 13F shows a new position AND the stock is showing unusual options activity or volume, the thesis is likely still active.

## Practical Tracking

EDGAR provides all filings for free (sec.gov/cgi-bin/browse-edgar). WhaleWisdom, Dataroma, and whalewisdom.com aggregate and diff filings automatically. The agent should pull 13F diffs quarterly and flag: new positions from top 20 managers, complete exits, and any position where 3+ top managers independently initiated in the same quarter (convergence signal).

## What 13Fs Cannot Tell You

Hedge funds with under $100M do not file. Short positions are invisible. The filing covers US exchange-listed equities only. Confidential treatment requests (SEC Rule 24b-2) allow funds to temporarily hide positions — Buffett has used this multiple times when building large stakes.
