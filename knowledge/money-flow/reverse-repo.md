# Reverse Repo Facility — The Fed's Liquidity Parking Lot

## What It Is

The Federal Reserve's Overnight Reverse Repurchase Agreement (ON RRP) facility allows eligible counterparties — primarily money market funds, government-sponsored enterprises, and banks — to deposit cash with the Fed overnight in exchange for Treasury securities. The Fed pays interest on these deposits at the ON RRP rate (set at the bottom of the Fed funds target range).

In plain terms: institutions park excess cash at the Fed and earn a guaranteed, risk-free return. The money sits there instead of flowing into markets.

## The 2021-2023 Explosion

After massive COVID-era quantitative easing flooded the system with liquidity, the ON RRP facility ballooned from near zero in early 2021 to a peak of approximately $2.55 trillion in December 2022. This was over $2.5 trillion in cash that CHOSE to sit at the Fed earning risk-free interest rather than chase returns in markets.

Why? The Fed's rate hikes made the ON RRP rate attractive enough that money market funds preferred the guaranteed return over short-term Treasuries or other money market instruments. Risk-free yield beat the hassle and marginal risk of alternatives.

## The Drain and Why It Matters

Starting in 2023, the ON RRP balance began declining as Treasury bill issuance surged (the Treasury needed to refill the TGA after the debt ceiling resolution). Money market funds shifted from the RRP into higher-yielding T-bills.

By late 2024, the ON RRP had drained to roughly $100-200 billion — releasing over $2 trillion back into the financial system. By early 2025, it stabilized at low levels.

This drain was one of the most significant liquidity injections in recent market history, and almost nobody talked about it. That $2+ trillion did not disappear. It flowed into:

1. **Treasury bills first** — absorbing massive government issuance
2. **Then longer-duration bonds** — as T-bill yields compressed
3. **Then risk assets** — equities, credit, crypto

The 2023-2024 stock market rally happened WHILE the Fed was still at restrictive rates. Many attributed it to AI enthusiasm. The mechanical reality: $2+ trillion of parked liquidity was re-entering the financial system.

## How to Track It

The New York Fed publishes ON RRP usage daily (newyorkfed.org/markets/desk-operations/reverse-repo). The number updates each afternoon.

What to watch:
- **Rapid drainage (>$50B/week):** liquidity is flooding back into markets. Bullish for risk assets.
- **Stabilization near zero:** the liquidity tailwind is exhausted. The easy fuel is spent. Future rallies need a different catalyst.
- **Any re-accumulation:** money is fleeing back to safety. Risk-off signal. This would likely coincide with stress in money markets or a flight to quality.

## The Bigger Picture

The ON RRP is one component of the overall liquidity equation: Fed balance sheet minus TGA (Treasury General Account) minus RRP roughly equals net liquidity in the system. All three variables matter. The agent should track net liquidity as a composite, not any single component in isolation.

When net liquidity rises, risk assets tend to follow with a 2-6 week lag. When net liquidity falls, risk assets eventually follow. This is not a timing tool — it is a regime indicator. It tells you whether the tide is coming in or going out.
