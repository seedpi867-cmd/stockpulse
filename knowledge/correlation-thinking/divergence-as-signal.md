# Divergence as Signal: When Correlated Assets Disagree, Someone Is Wrong

## Core Principle

When two assets that normally move together start diverging, one of them is mispriced. This is the single highest-signal pattern in markets. Divergences do not persist — they resolve, and the resolution creates a tradeable move. Your job is to figure out which asset is right and position for the convergence.

## Why Divergences Matter

Normal correlations exist because the assets share fundamental drivers. Stocks and copper both respond to economic growth. Gold and the dollar both respond to monetary policy. When they diverge, it means one market is pricing in a different future than the other. Since they cannot both be right about contradictory futures, one of them will adjust.

## Key Divergences to Monitor

**Stocks making new highs but copper making new lows:** Stocks are wrong. Copper reflects real industrial demand. If the economy is strong enough for new stock highs, copper demand should be rising. When copper diverges to the downside, it is telling you that the real economy is weaker than stock prices suggest. Resolution: stocks come down to meet copper, usually within 1-3 months.

**Dollar strengthening AND gold strengthening simultaneously:** This is unusual — normally they move inversely. When both strengthen together, it means fear is extreme. Both are "safety" assets, and capital is flowing into ALL safe havens regardless of their normal relationship. This signals a major risk event either happening or anticipated. Watch for: what is the source of fear? How will it resolve?

**Oil falling but energy stocks holding:** The equity market expects oil to recover. Energy stock investors are looking through the temporary weakness. This divergence usually resolves with oil bouncing back. But if the divergence persists beyond 2-3 weeks, the stocks are wrong and will eventually follow oil down.

**VIX falling while put/call ratio rises:** Options traders are buying protection (high put/call) but implied volatility is dropping (low VIX). Someone is wrong. Usually the put buyers are smarter — they are institutional hedgers with information. A rising put/call ratio despite falling VIX often precedes a volatility spike.

**Bond yields rising but bank stocks falling:** Banks profit from higher rates. If yields are rising and bank stocks are not responding, the bond market is pricing in something bad (credit risk, recession) that overwhelms the margin benefit. Trust the banks — they know their own loan books.

**Small caps underperforming while large caps rally:** The Russell 2000 is more economically sensitive than the S&P 500. When small caps diverge to the downside, it means the domestic economy is weakening even as mega-cap tech powers the indices higher. This divergence has preceded every major correction in the last two decades.

## The Resolution Process

Divergences resolve in one of three ways:

1. **Asset A corrects toward Asset B.** The overextended one snaps back.
2. **Asset B catches up to Asset A.** The laggard was just slow.
3. **Both move to a new equilibrium.** The old correlation regime has changed.

The first two are tradeable. The third means you need to update your correlation model.

## How to Trade Divergences

**Identify the divergence:** Two normally correlated assets are moving in opposite directions or one is flat while the other trends.

**Determine which is "right":** The asset with more fundamental support is usually right. Copper is more "real" than stocks. Credit is smarter than equity. Insider buying is smarter than price action.

**Wait for early confirmation:** Do not trade the divergence the moment you spot it. Wait for the first sign of resolution — the wrong asset starting to reverse. This reduces the risk of the divergence persisting longer than expected.

**Position for convergence:** Go long the underperformer or short the overperformer, or both as a pairs trade. Pairs trades reduce directional risk — you profit from the convergence regardless of whether both go up or both go down.

## Critical Warning

Divergences can persist longer than your capital can survive. Always use stops. A divergence that lasts three months is a signal. A divergence that lasts six months might be a regime change. Know the difference.
