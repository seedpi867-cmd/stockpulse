# The Fear-Greed Cycle

## The Emotional Arc of Markets

Markets cycle through predictable emotional phases that drive prices beyond what fundamentals justify in both directions. Understanding where the market sits in this cycle is worth more than any technical indicator because it reveals what other participants are likely to do next.

## The Greed Phase (Bull Market Progression)

**Stage 1 — Disbelief:** After a significant decline, early gains are met with skepticism. "It is a dead cat bounce." "Sell the rally." Short interest remains high. This is where the best risk/reward exists but where the fewest people are willing to buy. The S&P 500 rally from March 2009 saw massive outflows from equity funds for the first 12 months despite a 65% gain.

**Stage 2 — Hope:** Price action confirms the uptrend. Former skeptics start covering shorts. Volume picks up. Media narratives shift from apocalyptic to cautiously optimistic. Institutional investors begin increasing equity allocations.

**Stage 3 — Optimism:** The majority accept the bull market is real. Retail participation increases. IPO activity picks up. Valuations expand as investors project current growth rates into the future. The CNN Fear & Greed Index consistently reads above 60.

**Stage 4 — Euphoria:** Everyone is a genius. Taxi drivers and social media influencers give stock tips. Leverage reaches extremes. SPACs, meme stocks, and speculative names lead the market. The phrase "this time is different" appears in serious publications. Margin debt hit record $936 billion in October 2021, right before the 2022 bear market began.

## The Fear Phase (Bear Market Progression)

**Stage 5 — Anxiety:** Early decline is dismissed as healthy correction. "Buy the dip" is consensus. But dips get bought at lower and lower levels. Leadership narrows to defensive sectors.

**Stage 6 — Denial:** Investors hold losing positions, anchoring to their cost basis. "It will come back." Portfolio reviews are avoided. Unrealized losses are not treated as real.

**Stage 7 — Panic:** Forced selling begins. Margin calls trigger cascading liquidation. Correlations spike to 1.0 as everything sells together. The VIX spikes above 40. Liquidity evaporates in credit markets.

**Stage 8 — Capitulation:** The final washout. Investors sell at any price just to stop the pain. Volume spikes to extremes. This is the point of maximum financial opportunity but minimum psychological willingness to act. In March 2020, equity funds saw $40 billion in outflows during the week of the exact bottom.

## Practical Trading Application

The CNN Fear & Greed Index, AAII Sentiment Survey, put/call ratios, and VIX term structure provide quantitative measures of where we sit in the cycle. Extreme greed readings (above 80) have preceded an average 3-month S&P 500 return of -1.2%, while extreme fear readings (below 20) have preceded average 3-month returns of +8.5%. The edge is not in predicting the turn precisely but in sizing positions larger at fear extremes and smaller (or hedged) at greed extremes.
