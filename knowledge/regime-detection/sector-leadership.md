# Sector Leadership — What the Market Is Telling You

## The Principle

Which sectors lead the market tells you the market's dominant thesis. Sector rotation is not random — it reflects where the aggregate intelligence of millions of participants believes the economy and opportunities are heading. Reading sector leadership correctly is like reading the market's mind.

## The Leadership Signals

### Technology Leading
Thesis: growth, innovation, secular trends (AI, cloud, digital transformation). The market is optimistic about the future and willing to pay premium multiples for growth. Usually accompanied by falling or low real interest rates. When tech leads for an extended period, check whether it is broad-based tech or concentrated in a few names (see breadth-divergence.md).

### Energy Leading
Thesis: inflation, commodity supercycle, supply constraints. The market is pricing in higher input costs and favoring real assets over financial assets. Energy leadership often coincides with rising inflation expectations, rising oil prices, and underperformance of long-duration growth stocks. If energy leads while everything else lags, the market is saying inflation is the dominant risk.

### Financials Leading
Thesis: economic optimism, rising rates (steepening yield curve), credit expansion. Banks profit from the spread between short-term borrowing and long-term lending. When financials lead, the market believes the economy is strengthening, loan demand is growing, and credit conditions are favorable. Financial leadership early in a recovery is one of the most bullish sector signals.

### Utilities Leading
Thesis: fear, defensive positioning, hunt for yield. Utilities are the "hide and collect dividends" trade. When utilities outperform, the market is scared. Utility leadership alongside declining Treasury yields is a classic risk-off signal. If utilities are the best-performing sector for more than a few weeks, the market is bracing for trouble.

### Healthcare Leading
Thesis: defensive with growth characteristics. Healthcare tends to lead during late-cycle uncertainty when investors want defensiveness but are not yet panicking. It also leads when specific catalysts (drug approvals, biotech innovation) create sector-specific momentum.

### Consumer Discretionary Leading
Thesis: consumer confidence, spending strength. This sector includes retail, autos, housing, and leisure. When consumers are spending, the economy is healthy. Discretionary leadership is a mid-cycle signal of economic momentum.

### Consumer Staples Leading
Thesis: pure defense. When Procter & Gamble and Coca-Cola are the best performers, the market is in "preserve capital" mode. Staples leadership alongside utility leadership is maximum defensiveness — expect a downturn.

### Industrials Leading
Thesis: economic expansion, infrastructure spending, reshoring. Industrial leadership reflects expectations of physical economic activity — manufacturing, construction, transportation. It is a mid-to-late-cycle signal.

### Materials Leading
Thesis: inflation, global growth, commodity demand. Materials tend to lead when China is stimulating (massive infrastructure demand) or when inflation expectations are rising. Often leads alongside energy in a commodity-driven regime.

## Leadership Transitions

The real signal is when leadership CHANGES. A rotation from tech to energy tells you the market's thesis is shifting from growth to inflation. A rotation from cyclicals (discretionary, industrials) to defensives (utilities, staples) tells you the market expects a slowdown.

Track the 30-day and 90-day relative performance of the 11 GICS sectors vs the S&P 500. When a new sector takes the top position after being bottom-half for the prior period, the thesis is changing.

## For the Agent

- Calculate rolling 30-day sector ETF returns relative to SPY weekly
- Flag any sector that moves from bottom-3 to top-3 over a 60-day period (leadership rotation signal)
- Monitor the defensive vs cyclical ratio (XLU+XLP+XLV vs XLY+XLI+XLB) — rising ratio = market getting defensive, falling ratio = market getting aggressive
- Cross-reference sector leadership with VIX regime and breadth data for a complete market health assessment
