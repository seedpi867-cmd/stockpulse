# Crypto Trading Knowledge

## Key Differences from Equities
- 24/7 market — no close, no overnight gap risk, but also no rest
- Higher volatility — BTC routinely moves 5-10% in a day
- Correlation regime: BTC often correlates with NASDAQ/risk-on but decouples during crypto-specific events
- Liquidity is thinner outside US hours — wider spreads, bigger wicks
- No earnings, no dividends — price driven by flows, narrative, macro, and on-chain metrics

## BTC Setups Worth Trading
- **Macro breakout**: BTC breaks above multi-week range on rising volume after Fed dovish signal
- **Risk-on confirmation**: BTC + QQQ + falling VIX all moving together = strong trend
- **Weekend divergence**: BTC moves strongly on weekend while futures are closed — Monday gap play
- **Halving cycle positioning**: BTC tends to rally 12-18 months post-halving (last: Apr 2024)
- **ETF flow momentum**: Spot BTC ETF inflows/outflows signal institutional sentiment

## ETH Setups
- **ETH/BTC ratio**: When ETH/BTC is at cycle lows and BTC is stable, ETH tends to catch up
- **DeFi rotation**: ETH outperforms when gas fees drop and DeFi TVL rises
- **Upgrade catalysts**: Major Ethereum upgrades (Pectra, etc.) create buy-the-rumor setups

## Risk Rules for Crypto
- Position size: smaller than equities due to higher vol (max 5% of portfolio per crypto position)
- Wider stops: ATR-based, not fixed percentage — BTC needs 3-5% stops minimum
- Take partial profits at 2:1 risk-reward
- Never hold max position through major macro events (FOMC, CPI)
- Trailing stops work well in crypto trends — use ATR-based trails

## What to Watch
- BTC dominance (rising = risk-off within crypto, falling = altseason)
- Funding rates on perpetuals (extreme positive = overleveraged longs)
- Spot ETF daily flows (IBIT, FBTC, ARKB)
- Stablecoin supply (growing = dry powder, shrinking = exits)
- DXY correlation — strong dollar usually bearish crypto
