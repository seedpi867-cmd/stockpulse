# Correlation Breakdown Trading — When Pairs Diverge, Something Is Wrong

## The Principle

Certain assets are fundamentally linked. Gold and real interest rates. Oil prices and energy stock performance. Semiconductor stocks and the broader NASDAQ. Treasury yields and bank stocks. These pairs typically move together (or inversely) because they share underlying economic drivers.

When a normally correlated pair DIVERGES — when one moves significantly while the other does not follow — one of two things is happening:

1. **Temporary dislocation.** Market inefficiency, fund flows, or technical positioning has caused a temporary divergence. The pair will reconverge. This is the classic pairs trade opportunity.

2. **Regime change.** The fundamental relationship is breaking because the economic environment has changed. The divergence is an early warning signal of a larger shift. This is not a trade — it is intelligence.

Determining which scenario is playing out is the core challenge, and the edge.

## Key Pairs to Monitor

### Gold vs Real Yields (10-Year TIPS yield)
Normal relationship: inverse. When real yields rise (tighter money), gold falls. When real yields fall (looser money), gold rises. Gold prices and real yields have had a strong negative correlation for decades.

Divergence signal: If gold rallies while real yields ALSO rise, something unusual is happening. It could signal central bank gold buying (structural demand shift), currency crisis fears, or a breakdown in the dollar-gold relationship. This divergence occurred in 2022-2024 as central banks (particularly China, Poland, and others) bought gold aggressively despite rising real yields.

### Oil vs Energy Stocks (XLE)
Normal relationship: strongly positive. Energy stocks are leveraged plays on oil prices. When oil rises, XLE rises. When oil falls, XLE falls.

Divergence signal: If oil rises but energy stocks lag, the market is saying the oil move is temporary — perhaps a geopolitical spike that won't sustain. If energy stocks rally but oil is flat, the market is pricing in future oil strength or valuing energy companies for non-oil reasons (dividends, buybacks, capital discipline).

### Semiconductors (SOX Index) vs NASDAQ
Normal relationship: semiconductors lead NASDAQ. Semis are the picks-and-shovels of the tech economy. When semis outperform, it indicates strong underlying tech demand.

Divergence signal: If NASDAQ rallies but semis lag, the tech rally is driven by narrative (hype) rather than fundamental demand. If semis rally but NASDAQ lags, it suggests real tech capex is happening even if sentiment is poor — often a setup for a NASDAQ catch-up rally.

### Treasury Yields vs Bank Stocks (XLF)
Normal relationship: positive with steeper yield curve. Banks earn more when long-term rates are higher than short-term rates (net interest margin). A steepening yield curve is bullish for banks.

Divergence signal: If yields rise but banks fall, the market is worried about credit quality — rising rates are hurting borrowers and increasing default risk. This divergence preceded bank stress in 2023 (Silicon Valley Bank collapse).

### Copper vs S&P 500
Normal relationship: positive. Copper is "Dr. Copper" — an industrial metal whose demand reflects global economic activity. When copper rises, it generally confirms economic strength.

Divergence signal: If copper falls while stocks rally, the physical economy is weakening even as financial markets remain optimistic. This divergence often precedes equity weakness by 2-4 months.

## Trading the Divergence

**If expecting reconvergence (pairs trade):**
- Go long the lagging asset and short the leading asset
- Size based on historical divergence ranges — enter when divergence exceeds 2 standard deviations from the mean
- Set a time stop — if reconvergence does not occur within 30-60 days, the divergence may be structural, not temporary

**If suspecting regime change:**
- Do NOT trade the pair for convergence
- Instead, use the divergence as an input to regime detection (see regime-detection files)
- Adjust broader portfolio positioning based on what the divergence is signaling

## For the Agent

Track 5-7 key pairs with daily correlation monitoring. Flag any pair whose 20-day rolling correlation deviates by more than 2 standard deviations from its 252-day (1-year) average. For each flagged divergence, classify as likely temporary (favor pairs trade) or potentially structural (favor regime reassessment) based on whether a fundamental catalyst explains the break.
