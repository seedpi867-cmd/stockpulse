# Confirmation Bias: The Echo Chamber in Your Analysis

## The Trap

You are long NVDA. You read a bullish article about AI demand and it confirms your thesis. You feel smart. A bearish article about semiconductor cyclicality crosses your feed — you skim it, find a flaw in the argument, and dismiss it. You just engaged in confirmation bias: the tendency to seek, interpret, and remember information that confirms what you already believe while ignoring or discounting information that contradicts it.

Confirmation bias is universal and automatic. It operates below conscious awareness. You do not decide to ignore the bear case — your brain filters it out to protect your ego and your existing position from the discomfort of cognitive dissonance. In trading, where being wrong costs real money, this automatic filtering can be financially devastating.

## How It Operates in Markets

Research selection: after taking a position, traders overwhelmingly consume research that supports their thesis. A study by Park, Konana, Gu, Kumar, and Raghunathan (2013) found that investors actively sought out confirming information for their holdings and avoided disconfirming information, even when the disconfirming information was freely available.

Earnings interpretation: the same earnings report is interpreted differently by bulls and bears. Bulls focus on the revenue beat; bears focus on the margin compression. Both are in the same report. Confirmation bias determines which data point feels more important.

Analyst selection: traders follow analysts who agree with them and dismiss those who do not. "That analyst is always wrong" usually means "that analyst disagrees with my position."

Selective memory: after a trade works, you remember the analysis that led to it. After it fails, you remember the bad luck that caused it. Over time, this creates a false sense of analytical skill — you remember your insights but not your errors.

## The Cost

Confirmation bias causes traders to hold positions too long because they keep finding reasons to stay in. It prevents them from cutting losses because the bear case never feels compelling. It leads to concentrated positions because every piece of confirming evidence increases conviction. And it blinds them to regime changes — the moment when the thesis breaks — because the contradicting signals are being filtered out.

The most dangerous form is thesis drift. You bought a stock for its dividend yield. It cuts the dividend. Instead of selling (the original thesis is dead), you find a new reason to hold — "the growth story is actually more interesting." This is confirmation bias manufacturing justifications to avoid the pain of admitting you were wrong.

## The Professional Antidote

Pre-mortem analysis: before entering a trade, write down what would have to happen for the trade to fail. Define the kill criteria in advance. When those conditions are met, exit regardless of how the confirming evidence feels.

Steel-man the opposition: for every position, actively seek the strongest bear case (if long) or bull case (if short). Read analysis from people who disagree with you. If you cannot articulate the opposing view convincingly, you do not understand your own position well enough.

Devil's advocate process: some hedge funds designate a team member to argue against every trade idea. The person's job is to find flaws. This institutionalises contrarian thinking and overrides the group's natural confirmation bias.

Track your reasoning: write down why you entered a trade and what you expect to happen. Review these notes when exiting. This creates accountability for your pre-trade thesis and prevents retroactive narrative construction.

## What the Agent Should Know

The agent should systematically generate both bull and bear cases for every position and weight them equally regardless of existing exposure. When presenting analysis, the agent should flag confirming and disconfirming evidence separately. The agent should track thesis integrity — when the original reason for a trade no longer holds, the trade should be re-evaluated from scratch, not supported with new justifications.
