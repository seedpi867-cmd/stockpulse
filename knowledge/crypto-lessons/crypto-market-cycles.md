# Crypto Market Cycles: Stock Market Psychology at 10x Speed

## Why Crypto Cycles Matter for Stock Trading

Cryptocurrency markets are the purest expression of speculative market psychology that exists. They are largely unregulated, emotionally driven, accessible 24/7, and populated by retail investors with minimal institutional guardrails. Every psychological pattern that stocks exhibit — greed, fear, bubble formation, capitulation, recovery — crypto exhibits more extremely and more quickly.

Understanding crypto cycles gives a stock market agent a high-speed laboratory for studying the same dynamics that play out in slower motion in equities.

## The Four-Year Bitcoin Halving Cycle

Bitcoin has a built-in supply shock every four years. The "halving" cuts the rate of new Bitcoin creation in half, reducing supply while demand continues. This has historically triggered a predictable cycle:

Year 0 (halving year): gradual price recovery and accumulation. Smart money enters. Prices slowly grind up as reduced supply takes effect.

Year 1 (post-halving): the bull run. Prices explode upward as narrative momentum attracts mainstream attention. Bitcoin goes from "boring" to "I need to buy this now." The 2017 and 2021 cycles saw 10-20x returns from the halving to peak.

Year 2 (blow-off top and crash): the bubble pops. Prices crash 70-85% from the peak. The narrative shifts from "paradigm shift" to "tulip mania." Media writes obituaries. The crash is driven by the same over-leverage and euphoria dynamics as every stock market crash, just faster.

Year 3 (accumulation): prices grind along at depressed levels. Most retail participants have left. Volume is low. The builders keep building. This is the equivalent of the stock market's "valley of despair" that follows every bear market.

The halvings occurred in 2012, 2016, 2020, and 2024. Each followed this approximate cycle.

## Altcoin Seasons

Within the crypto cycle, there is a secondary pattern: Bitcoin leads, then "altcoins" (everything other than Bitcoin) follow. The sequence is predictable.

Bitcoin rallies first, often for months, while altcoins lag. Bitcoin dominance (Bitcoin's share of total crypto market cap) rises. Once Bitcoin's rally slows and starts consolidating, speculative capital rotates into altcoins seeking higher returns. Altcoin dominance rises. The smallest, most speculative tokens rally the most — this is the "altcoin season." When the most absurd tokens (meme coins, celebrity tokens) are going vertical, the cycle is near its top.

This rotation pattern is identical to how stock market cycles work: large caps lead, then capital rotates to mid-caps, then small caps, then speculative micro-caps. When the most speculative tier is leading, the cycle is late.

## The Leverage Liquidation Cascade

Crypto markets have extremely high leverage available — 50x to 100x on some exchanges. This creates a unique crash dynamic: the liquidation cascade. When prices drop, leveraged positions are forcibly liquidated, which pushes prices lower, which triggers more liquidations. The cascading effect produces the 20-30% single-day crashes that are characteristic of crypto.

This exact mechanism exists in stock markets through margin calls and forced selling, but crypto demonstrates it in its purest form. The lesson: leverage amplifies both gains and losses, and in a crowded leveraged trade, the unwind is always faster and more violent than the build-up.

## What the Agent Should Know

Crypto cycles are accelerated stock market cycles. The emotional progression — disbelief, hope, optimism, euphoria, complacency, anxiety, denial, panic, capitulation, anger, depression, disbelief (again) — is identical. The agent should use crypto market behaviour as a leading indicator for broader risk appetite. When crypto is in euphoria mode (meme coins exploding, leverage at highs, YouTube influencers recommending altcoins), equity markets are likely in a late-cycle speculative phase. When crypto is in capitulation, risk appetite across all markets is probably contracting.
