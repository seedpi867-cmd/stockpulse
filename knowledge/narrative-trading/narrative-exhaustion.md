# Narrative Exhaustion — Every Story Has an Expiration Date

## The Lifecycle

Every market narrative follows a predictable arc:

**Phase 1 — Discovery (the edge).** A small group of investors identifies a structural change or opportunity. They build positions quietly. Media coverage is minimal. Skepticism is the default. This is where the real money is made, and it is virtually impossible to identify in real time because the narrative has not yet been articulated.

**Phase 2 — Adoption (the easy money).** The narrative gains traction. Analyst initiations. Conference panels. Early media coverage. Institutional funds begin allocating. Price appreciation accelerates. The narrative has a name and a thesis. This is the sweet spot — enough confirmation to have confidence, enough skepticism remaining to provide a wall of worry for prices to climb.

**Phase 3 — Consensus (the warning signs).** The narrative is universally accepted. "Everyone knows AI is the future." CNBC runs daily segments. Retail investors pile in. New ETFs and products launch to capture the theme. Contrarians are mocked. At this point, the narrative is fully priced in and possibly overpriced. The easy money has been made.

**Phase 4 — Exhaustion (the danger zone).** New converts slow to a trickle because everyone who is going to buy has already bought. Marginal buyers are gone. Any disappointment triggers outsized selling because there is no new money to absorb it. The narrative is not dead — it is just exhausted. The stories are the same, the facts may still support it, but the capital is fully deployed.

**Phase 5 — Death (the contrarian opportunity).** Media coverage turns negative or disappears entirely. Investors who were enthusiastic 6 months ago are embarrassed and do not want to discuss it. Prices overshoot to the downside as liquidation pressure exceeds any remaining believers. THIS is often the best contrarian entry for the next cycle.

## Detecting Exhaustion

**Ubiquity indicators:**
- When taxi drivers, hairdressers, or your non-financial friends are discussing the trade, Phase 3 is mature and Phase 4 is approaching. This is the classic Shoeshine Boy indicator, attributed to Joseph Kennedy before the 1929 crash.
- When every company in an unrelated industry starts claiming exposure to the theme (every company is an "AI company" in 2024), the narrative has become corporate marketing, not investment thesis.
- When new financial products launch to capture the theme (thematic ETFs, SPACs focused on the sector), institutions are packaging the narrative for retail sale — which means institutional money is looking to exit.

**Flow indicators:**
- ETF inflows for the thematic sector decelerating despite continued media coverage
- Insider selling in narrative-leading companies while retail buying continues
- Short interest rising in narrative leaders — early smart money is positioning for the turn

**Price indicators:**
- Stocks in the narrative sector react negatively to good news (beat earnings but sell off) — the good news is already priced in
- New information no longer moves prices — the narrative is saturated
- Sector underperforms the broad market despite no change in fundamentals

## The Contrarian Cycle

Narrative death creates the setup for the next opportunity. When nobody wants to own a sector, valuations compress to levels that provide genuine fundamental value. The contrarian buy signal is not when the narrative is dead — it is when it is BURIED. When media has stopped covering the sector entirely, when funds have closed, when analysts have dropped coverage.

Energy in 2020 was a perfect example. The narrative was dead — "peak oil demand," "stranded assets," "ESG will kill fossil fuels." Energy stocks traded at the lowest valuations in decades. Then the commodity cycle turned, and energy was the best-performing sector of 2021-2022.

## For the Agent

- Track narrative lifecycle phase for each thematic investment thesis (AI, energy transition, reshoring, etc.)
- Monitor the exhaustion indicators: media frequency, ETF product launches, retail participation levels, insider selling
- When exhaustion indicators trigger for a current holding, begin systematic reduction regardless of whether the narrative "still makes sense"
- When death indicators trigger for a previously exhausted narrative, add to watchlist for potential contrarian entry
- Never be the last buyer of a consensus narrative — by the time it is consensus, the risk/reward is inverted
