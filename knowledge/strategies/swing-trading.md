# Swing Trading Fundamentals

## Definition and Timeframe

Swing trading targets moves lasting 2-10 days, sitting between day trading (intraday) and position trading (weeks to months). The goal is capturing the "meat" of a price swing — not the exact bottom or top, but the 60-70% in the middle. This timeframe suits traders who cannot watch screens all day but want more activity than buy-and-hold.

## Core Setup: The Pullback Entry

The highest-probability swing trade is buying a pullback in an uptrend (or shorting a bounce in a downtrend). The setup requires three conditions:

1. **Trend confirmation:** Stock above its 50-day and 200-day moving averages, with the 50 above the 200. ADX above 25 confirms trending conditions.
2. **Orderly pullback:** Price retraces to a support level (20-day EMA, prior breakout level, or Fibonacci 38.2-61.8% retracement) on declining volume. This indicates profit-taking, not distribution.
3. **Reversal candle:** A hammer, bullish engulfing, or morning star pattern at support with volume pickup signals buyers stepping in.

Enter on the next day open after the reversal candle. Stop goes below the pullback low. Target is 2-3x the risk (the prior swing high for the first target, measured move for the second).

## Volume Analysis

Volume is the swing trader most important confirmation tool. On a valid pullback: volume should contract as price pulls back and expand as it resumes the trend. If volume increases during the pullback, institutions may be distributing rather than allowing a normal retracement — avoid the trade.

Look for volume spikes at key support levels — this often indicates institutional buying. A stock pulling back to its 50-day MA on 0.5x average volume, then printing a reversal candle on 2x volume, is a textbook swing entry.

## Risk Management Framework

- **Position size:** Risk 1% of account per trade. If your stop is 5% below entry, position size = 20% of account.
- **Maximum open positions:** 4-6 concurrent swing trades to diversify without over-diluting attention.
- **Correlated positions:** Never hold more than 2 positions in the same sector. A surprise earnings miss from one tech stock can drag all tech positions simultaneously.
- **Partial profits:** Take 50% off at 1:1 risk/reward, trail the rest with a stop at breakeven.

## Scanning Criteria

Effective swing trade scans look for: relative strength rank above 80 (stock outperforming 80% of the market), pulling back 3-8% from a 20-day high, RSI(14) between 40-50 (not oversold, just pulled back from strength), and average volume above 500K shares for liquidity. This typically yields 5-15 candidates per day in a normal market.

## Common Mistakes

Entering before the reversal candle confirms wastes capital in further decline. Holding through earnings is gambling, not swing trading — always exit or hedge before the report. Ignoring the broader market trend accounts for 50%+ of swing trade failures; even the best setups fail when the S&P 500 is breaking down.
