# Insider Buying as a Contrarian Signal

## Why Insider Buying Matters

Corporate insiders — CEOs, CFOs, directors, and officers holding more than 10% of shares — are required to report their trades to the SEC within two business days via Form 4 filings. These filings are public and provide a legal window into what the people with the most intimate knowledge of a company think about its prospects.

The asymmetry is critical: insiders sell for many reasons (diversification, taxes, estate planning, home purchases), but they buy for only one reason — they believe the stock is undervalued. This makes insider buying a far more reliable signal than insider selling.

## The Statistical Edge

Academic research consistently supports insider buying as a predictive signal:

- Lakonishok and Lee (2001) found that stocks with heavy insider buying outperformed the market by 7.5% annually over the following 12 months.
- Seyhun (1998) analyzed 40 years of insider trading data and found that companies with three or more insiders buying simultaneously outperformed by 10% or more over the next year.
- Insider purchases at 52-week lows have an even stronger track record — these represent insiders buying when sentiment is worst, which is the purest contrarian signal.

## What to Look For

**Cluster buying:** A single insider buying is informative. Three or more insiders buying within the same month is a much stronger signal — it indicates a shared conviction that cannot be explained by individual circumstances.

**CEO and CFO purchases:** Not all insiders are equal. The CEO and CFO have the most complete picture of company fundamentals. A Form 4 showing the CEO buying 50,000 dollars or more of stock in the open market (not through options exercises) is a top-tier signal.

**Size relative to compensation:** A director buying 10,000 dollars of stock when their annual compensation is 300,000 is noise. That same director buying 500,000 dollars is putting real skin in the game. Look for purchases that represent a meaningful percentage of the insider total net worth or annual income.

**Buying during drawdowns:** Insiders who buy after a 30-40% decline in their company stock are signaling that the selloff has overshot fundamentals. This is the strongest setup — combine it with other value metrics (price-to-book below historical average, free cash flow yield above 8%) for highest conviction.

## Where to Find the Data

SEC EDGAR provides all Form 4 filings. Aggregator sites like OpenInsider, InsiderMonkey, and Finviz filter and rank insider activity. Set up alerts for cluster buying (3+ insiders in 30 days) in mid-cap stocks (2-10 billion dollar market cap) — this is the sweet spot where insider information advantage is highest and the stock is liquid enough to trade.

## Caveats

Insider buying is not infallible. Insiders were buying Enron, Lehman Brothers, and SVB Financial on the way down. The signal works best as one factor among many — combine it with fundamental value, technical support, and macro conditions. Also be aware of 10b5-1 pre-planned trading plans, which are automated purchases that do not reflect real-time conviction. Check whether the purchase was a discretionary open-market buy or part of a pre-existing plan.
